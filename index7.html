Uranus

When Discovered:

Uranus was officially discovered by William Herschel on March 13, 1781. It was the first planet discovered using a telescope, though it had been mistaken for a star in earlier observations.

Size:

  • Diameter: 50,724 kilometers (31,518 miles)
  • Uranus is the third-largest planet in the solar system by diameter, after Jupiter and Saturn.

Atmosphere:

  • Uranus has a thick atmosphere primarily composed of :
  • 1. Hydrogen (82%)
  • 2. Helium (15%)
  • 3. Methane (2%)
  • The methane in the atmosphere absorbs red light and gives Uranus its blue-green color.

Distance from Sun and Earth:

  • From the Sun: Approximately 2.87 billion kilometers (1.78 billion miles).
  • From the Earth: The average distance is about 2.6 billion kilometers (1.6 billion miles), varying with orbital positions.
