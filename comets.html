Comets

What is a Comet?

A comet is a small icy body that orbits the Sun. When it comes close to the Sun, its ice turns into gas, forming a glowing coma and often a bright tail that can stretch millions of kilometers.

Composition of a Comet

    Comets are made of:

  • 1. Nucleus – A solid core of ice, dust, and rock.
  • 2. Coma – A glowing halo of gas and dust around the nucleus.
  • 3. Tail – Formed when solar radiation pushes dust and gas away from the comet.

Famous Comets

  • 1. Halley's Comet – Visible every 76 years; next appearance in 2061.
  • 2. Hale-Bopp – One of the brightest comets, observed in 1997.
  • 3. Comet NEOWISE – Recently visible in 2020, captivating stargazers
