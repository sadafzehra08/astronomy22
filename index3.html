Venus

When Discovered:

Venus has been known since ancient times due to its brightness in the sky. It is visible to the naked eye and often referred to as the "Morning Star" or "Evening Star."

Size:

  • Diameter: 12,104 kilometers (7,521 miles)
  • Venus is slightly smaller than Earth, making it the sixth-largest planet in the solar system.

Atmosphere:

  • Venus has a thick and toxic atmosphere composed mostly of :
  • 1. Carbon Dioxide (96.5%)
  • 2. Nitrogen (3.5%)
  • Traces of Sulfur Dioxide and other gases.
  • The dense atmosphere causes a runaway greenhouse effect, making Venus the hottest planet in the solar system with surface temperatures reaching up to 475°C (900°F).

Distance from Sun and Earth:

  • From the Sun: Approximately 108.2 million kilometers (67.2 million miles).
  • From the Earth: The average distance is about 41 million kilometers (25 million miles) when the two planets are closest.
