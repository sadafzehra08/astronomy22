Evolution OF Earth

Formation of Earth

About 4.5 billion years ago, Earth formed from a cloud of gas and dust around the Sun. Gravity pulled the materials together to create a hot, molten planet.

Cooling and Formation of Crust

As Earth cooled, a solid crust formed on its surface. Volcanic activity released gases, creating the early atmosphere.

Formation of Oceans

Water vapor in the atmosphere condensed, leading to rainfall that filled the low areas of the crust, forming the first oceans.

Beginning of Life

About 3.5 billion years ago, simple life forms, like bacteria, appeared in the oceans. These organisms released oxygen through photosynthesis, slowly changing the atmosphere.

Development of Complex Life

Over millions of years, life evolved into more complex forms, including plants, animals, and eventually humans. Continents also shifted and changed shape due to plate tectonics.

Present Day Earth

Today, Earth is a planet full of diverse life. It continues to evolve with natural changes and human impact.
