Jupiter

When Discovered:

Jupiter has been known since ancient times and was observed by many early civilizations, including the Babylonians around 7th or 8th century BCE. Galileo Galilei made significant discoveries about Jupiter in 1610 when he observed its four largest moons.

Size:

  • Diameter: 139,820 kilometers (86,881 miles)
  • Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system. It is so massive that it is 318 times heavier than Earth!

Atmosphere:

  • Jupiter has a thick atmosphere primarily composed of :
  • 1. Hydrogen (90%)
  • 2. Helium (10%)
  • Trace amounts of Methane, Ammonia, and other gases.
  • The planet's atmosphere is famous for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm larger than Earth that has been raging for centuries.

Distance from Sun and Earth:

  • From the Sun: Approximately 778.5 million kilometers (483.8 million miles).
  • From the Earth: On average, about 628 million kilometers (390 million miles), depending on the orbital positions of the two planets.
