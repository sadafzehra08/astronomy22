Saturn

When Discovered:

Saturn has been known since ancient times. It was observed by ancient civilizations like the Babylonians and Romans. Galileo Galilei was the first to observe Saturn's rings in 1610, though he could not identify them as rings due to the limitations of his telescope.

Size:

  • Diameter: 116,460 kilometers (72,366 miles)
  • Saturn is the second-largest planet in the solar system, after Jupiter.

Atmosphere:

  • Saturn's atmosphere is primarily composed of :
  • 1. Hydrogen (96.3%)
  • 2. Helium (3.25%)
  • Trace amounts of Methane, Ammonia, and other gases.
  • The atmosphere features high-speed winds and storms, and its yellowish appearance comes from the ammonia crystals in the upper atmosphere.

Distance from Sun and Earth:

  • From the Sun: Approximately 1.43 billion kilometers (886 million miles).
  • From the Earth: The average distance is about 1.2 billion kilometers (746 million miles), depending on orbital positions.
