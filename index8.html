Neptune

When Discovered:

Neptune was discovered on September 23, 1846 by Johann Galle, based on predictions by mathematicians Urbain Le Verrier and John Couch Adams. It was the first planet located through mathematical calculations rather than direct observation.

Size:

  • Diameter: 49,244 kilometers (30,598 miles)
  • Neptune is the fourth-largest planet in the solar system by diameter.

Atmosphere:

  • Neptune's atmosphere is primarily composed of :
  • 1. Hydrogen (80%)
  • 2. Helium (19%)
  • 3. Methane (1%)
  • Methane gives Neptune its deep blue color. The atmosphere is dynamic, with strong winds and massive storms, including the Great Dark Spot, similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot.

Distance from Sun and Earth:

  • From the Sun: Approximately 4.5 billion kilometers (2.8 billion miles).
  • From the Earth: The average distance is about 4.3 billion kilometers (2.7 billion miles), depending on orbital positions.
