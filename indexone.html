THE
ASTRONOMY WORLD

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects such as stars, planets, comets, and galaxies, as well as phenomena that occur outside Earth's atmosphere. It seeks to understand the origins, evolution, and behavior of the universe. By observing the night sky and using advanced technology, astronomers decode the mysteries of space and time. It combines principles from physics, chemistry, and mathematics to explain cosmic events. Through astronomy, we uncover humanity's place in the vast, ever-expanding universe.

Big Bang Theory

Big Bang Theory

The Big Bang theory is an explanation of the early development of the Universe. According to this theory the Universe expanded from an extremely small, extremely hot, and extremely dense state. Since then it has expanded and become less dense and cooler.

Evolution OF Earth

Evolution OF Earth

As the Earth started to grow, about 4.56 billion years ago, the heavy iron sank to the center, and the lighter silicates rose to the surface. Heat generated from impacts of other bodies kept the early Earth molten. In fact, the planet's entire outer layer may have been liquid, an idea that came from studying the Moon.

Solar System

Planets

Planets are celestial bodies that orbit a star, like the Sun in our solar system. Each planet is unique, varying in size, composition, and distance from its star. Some, like Earth, are rocky and support life, while others, like Jupiter, are massive gas giants with no solid surface.
