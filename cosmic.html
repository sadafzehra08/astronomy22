Comets

Comets are icy celestial objects that orbit the Sun, often described as "dirty snowballs" made of dust, rock, and frozen gases. When they approach the Sun, the heat causes their ice to vaporize, creating a glowing coma and a distinct tail that can stretch millions of kilometers.

Constillations

Constillations

Constellations are patterns of stars visible in the night sky, forming shapes that ancient civilizations associated with myths, animals, or objects. They serve as celestial maps, helping in navigation and identifying specific regions of the sky. Examples include Orion, known for its "hunter" shape and bright stars Betelgeuse and Rigel, and Ursa Major, famous for the Big Dipper asterism.
