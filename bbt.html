Big Bang Theory

What is the Big Bang Theory?

The Big Bang Theory explains how the universe began. It suggests that about 13.8 billion years ago, the universe started as a tiny, super-hot, and super-dense point.

How Did It Happen?

Suddenly, this point expanded very quickly, like a giant explosion. This expansion created space, time, and everything in the universe.

Formation of Stars and Galaxies

As the universe grew, it began to cool down. Over billions of years, stars, planets, and galaxies formed.

Evidence for the Big Bang

Scientists have found evidence, such as :

  • 1. Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation: Leftover heat from the Big Bang.
  • 2. Movement of Galaxies: Galaxies are moving away from each other, showing the universe is still expanding.

Why is it Important?

The Big Bang is not just an explosion. It marks the beginning of everything we see today. It helps us understand how the universe works and continues to grow.
