Latest Developments

Discovery of Exoplanets

Astronomers have found Earth-like exoplanets in habitable zones, with tools like the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) detecting water, methane, and other gases in their atmospheres.

(JWST) Observations

JWST has provided detailed images of protoplanetary disks, revealing how planets form around young stars and offering new data on distant celestial objects.

Understanding Stellar Life Cycles

Studies of supernovae and dying stars like Betelgeuse are uncovering critical details about the birth and death of stars and the physics of extreme cosmic events.

Probing Distant Galaxies and Star Clusters

Large-scale surveys are mapping star formations in galaxies, shedding light on how star clusters evolve and interact with their surroundings.

Planetary Science

Missions like Perseverance are searching for signs of past life on Mars, while studies of moons like Europa and Titan explore their potential to support life.

Citizen Science

Platforms like Zooniverse engage the public in classifying stars and finding planetary transits, contributing to significant discoveries in astronomy.
